The timeline of objects

Where did the virtual reality headset we know so well come from? Scroll through this page to find out.

2024
The virtual reality headset lets you dive into immersive 3D worlds, play games, visit places from a distance... The experience is visual and auditory only.

2051
The experience is more immersive, thanks to a wider field of vision, integrated high- definition screens and 360° sound reproduction. However, the result is the same: no sense of total

immersion.

2076
This type of headset would offer far more total immersion, thanks to a spherical shell that blocks external distractions and a 180° to 360° field of view via high-resolution OLED microdisplays. It would incorporate a 3D audio system for immersive

sound and advanced eye tracking for natural interactions.

2104
All these innovations led to what we know today: full-body virtual reality headsets. Covering the whole body, it works like a full immersive suit, offering a total sensory experience. It incorporates

full-surface haptic sensors to simulate touch, heat and vibration, as well as micro-screens for panoramic vision. The device precisely tracks body movements using gyroscopes and accelerometers, enabling the user to physically move and interact in the virtual environment.
